Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students

UPI Tip 1: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is only required when you are sending money or checking your balance.
UPI Tip 2: Always verify your actual bank account balance by logging into your official banking app before believing any SMS payment alerts.
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all critical email, social media, and university portal accounts.
Avoid conducting sensitive financial transactions while connected to public college Wi-Fi or unsecured cafe networks.
Use a reliable password manager instead of memorizing the same password for your student portal and your bank accounts.
Do not click on unsolicited links in emails or SMS claiming your account is blocked or needs an urgent "KYC update."
Keep your social media profiles private and avoid sharing your phone number, location, or daily schedule publicly.
Keep your laptop and smartphone operating systems updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.

Mandatory Reporting Channels:
If you or a peer encounter a cybercrime, report it immediately:
National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
National Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (Available 24x7 for financial fraud)
